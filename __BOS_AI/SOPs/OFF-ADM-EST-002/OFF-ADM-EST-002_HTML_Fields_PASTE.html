ℹ Why This SOP Exists

This SOP controls how estimators receive, build, and advance estimates through the BOS My Estimates board. It exists to ensure every estimate is worked promptly, scoped accurately, and recorded in BOS before it goes to the customer — eliminating missed site visits, vague proposals, and untracked outcomes.

✓ Applies To

  • All estimates assigned to the estimator via field_assigned_to
  • My Estimates board at /admin/office/estimates/my-estimates
  • All service types and estimate bundles
  • Scope summary authoring on all applicable estimate bundles

✕ Does Not Apply To

  • Office pipeline management (see OFF-EST-001)
  • Contract creation or work order generation
  • Estimate request intake — that is the office role
  • Accepted and Declined record management

field_sop_responsibilities — Rules & Responsibilities

Paste this HTML into
field_sop_responsibilities
MUST
  • Estimators must contact the customer within 1 business day of receiving an assigned estimate
  • Estimators must advance the estimate stage on the My Estimates board each time the real-world status changes
  • Estimators must visit the property before building the estimate — no remote pricing on site-dependent work
  • Estimators must update field_scope_summary with specific, accurate project details before advancing past In Preparation
  • Estimators must enter all line items (labor, materials, equipment) in BOS — not on paper
  • Estimators must record the customer's response (Accepted or Declined) in BOS immediately when it occurs
MUST NOT
  • Estimators must not leave an estimate in New stage for more than 1 business day without contacting the customer
  • Estimators must not advance past In Preparation with placeholder scope text — the system will block this
  • Estimators must not quote prices verbally without recording them in BOS
  • Estimators must not change the estimate request board status — that is the office role
  • Estimators must not mark an estimate Accepted or Declined without the customer's explicit response

The Estimate Stage Board at a Glance

Your My Estimates board groups all assigned estimates by stage. Use the ← Back and Next Stage → buttons on each row to advance stages without leaving the page.

1. New Assigned to you. Customer not yet contacted.
▼
2. Contacted You have made first contact with the customer.
▼
3. Appointment Set Site visit scheduled. Date and time confirmed.
▼
4. In Preparation Property visited. Building the estimate in BOS.
▼
5. Under Review Complete. Being reviewed internally before sending.
▼
6. Estimate Sent Proposal delivered to customer.
▼
7. Client Feedback Customer responded with questions or changes.
▼
8. Pending Customer is considering. No decision yet.
▼
9. Accepted ✓ Customer said yes. Moves off this board.
10. Declined ✕ Customer said no. Moves off this board.

Pre-Checks

  • Confirm you are logged into BOS with your estimator account
  • Go to My Estimates at /admin/office/estimates/my-estimates
  • Check the New swimlane — any estimate here needs to be picked up today
  • Check the ? help button on the My Estimates page for a reminder of what each stage means

Steps

1 Pick Up the Estimate Estimator
  • Go to My Estimates board — new assignments appear in the New swimlane
  • Click the estimate title to open it and read the job details
  • Read "Client Requested the Following" — this is what the office captured from the customer call
  • Check the property address and confirm you can access the site
  • Do not advance the stage until you have reviewed the job details
2 Contact the Customer — advance to Contacted Estimator
  • Call the customer to introduce yourself and discuss the request
  • Confirm what they are looking for — do not assume the office notes cover everything
  • Once you have made first contact, click "Contacted →" on the My Estimates board row
  • If the customer is unreachable, attempt contact at least twice before flagging to the office
3 Schedule the Site Visit — advance to Appointment Set Estimator
  • Confirm a date and time for the site visit with the customer
  • Click "Appointment Set →" once date and time are confirmed
  • Note any access requirements (gate codes, dogs, call ahead) — record in estimate notes if relevant
4 Visit the Property — advance to In Preparation Estimator
  • Visit the property at the scheduled time
  • Take measurements, photos, and notes as needed
  • Assess scope, conditions, and constraints (drainage, slope, existing irrigation, etc.)
  • For simple maintenance jobs — you may quote on the spot
  • After the visit, click "In Preparation →" on the board
5 Build the Estimate in BOS Estimator
  • Open the estimate in BOS and add line items: labor, materials, equipment
  • Calculate totals — field_estimate_total updates from line items automatically
  • REQUIRED — Update the Scope Summary: replace all placeholder text with a real description of the work. See Scope Summary Rules below.
  • Double-check: does the total reflect what you would actually charge for this job?
6 Scope Summary — Required Before Advancing Estimator
  • The system will block you from advancing past In Preparation if the scope is still placeholder text
  • Open the estimate, find the Scope Summary field, and replace all auto-generated text
  • Write exactly what will be done: which areas, what materials, what methods, what is excluded
  • The scope is what the customer sees in the proposal — it must be accurate and specific

Scope Summary — Acceptable vs. Unacceptable

✕ Unacceptable ✓ Acceptable
"Landscaping project at this property."

"Lawn care service."

"Client wants some work done in the yard." 		"Remove existing river rock and weed mat in front bed (approx 400 sq ft). Install new weed barrier and 3" layer of brown river rock. Plant 4 arborvitae along north fence. Haul off all debris."
7 Review and Advance to Estimate Sent Estimator / Office
  • If the estimate needs a second opinion — advance to "Under Review →"
  • For straightforward jobs — advance directly to "Estimate Sent →" when ready
  • The office or estimator sends the proposal to the customer
  • Advance immediately when sent — do not leave in Estimate Sent
8 Handle Customer Feedback Estimator / Office
  • If customer responds with questions or revision requests — advance to "Client Feedback →"
  • Address their feedback, update the estimate if needed, and re-send
  • Advance back to "Estimate Sent →" after responding
  • If customer goes silent — advance to "Pending →" and notify the office to follow up
9 Record the Outcome Estimator / Office
  • Customer accepts → Click "Accepted →" on the board. For maintenance services, BOS automatically updates the linked contract section. For design-build, the office collects the deposit.
  • Customer declines → Click ✕ on the board row. Confirm the dialog. The estimate moves off the My Estimates board.
  • Never mark Accepted or Declined without the customer's explicit response

✕ System Enforcement

The system will not let you advance an estimate past "In Preparation" if the Scope Summary contains placeholder text or is empty. This is a hard block — not optional. Update the scope after your site visit, before you try to advance the stage.

Quality Checks

  • Every new estimate is picked up and moved to Contacted within 1 business day of assignment
  • No estimate sits in In Preparation with placeholder scope text
  • Every estimate sent to a customer has a specific, accurate Scope Summary
  • No verbal prices given without a corresponding BOS estimate entry
  • Accepted and Declined outcomes recorded in BOS the same day the customer responds

Completion

  • Estimate is in Accepted or Declined stage
  • All line items entered in BOS with correct totals
  • Scope Summary reflects the actual agreed-upon work
  • For Accepted: BOS has updated the linked contract section (maintenance) or deposit received confirmed (design-build)

Common Mistakes

✕ Don't ✓ Do
Leave an estimate in New for more than 1 day without calling Call the customer the same day you receive the assignment email
Leave scope as "CLIENT REQUEST (update after site visit…)" Update the scope after your site visit with real project details
Quote a price verbally without entering it into BOS Enter all estimates in BOS — verbal quotes that aren't recorded don't exist
Change the estimate request board status Only advance your estimate stages — the office manages the request board
Mark Accepted before the customer has actually said yes Only mark Accepted or Declined after an explicit customer response
